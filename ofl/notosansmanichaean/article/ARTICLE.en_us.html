Noto Sans Manichaean is an unmodulated (“sans serif”) design for texts in the historical Middle Eastern Manichaean script.

Noto Sans Manichaean contains 153 glyphs, 6 OpenType features, and supports 60 characters from the Unicode block Manichaean.

Supported writing systems

Manichaean

Manichaean is a historical Middle Eastern abjad, written right-to-left. Was used in the 3rd–10th century CE by the followers of Manichaeanism, an Iranian Gnostic religion, for Middle Iranian languages and for Old Uyghur. Needs software support for complex text layout (shaping). Read more on ScriptSource, Unicode, Wikipedia, Wiktionary, r12a.
