Noto Sans Tagalog is an unmodulated (“sans serif”) design for texts in the historical Southeast Asian Tagalog script.

Noto Sans Tagalog contains 31 glyphs, and supports 30 characters from the Unicode block Tagalog.

Supported writing systems

Tagalog

Tagalog (Baybayin, Alibata, ᜊᜌ᜔ᜊᜌᜒᜈ᜔) is a historical Southeast Asian abugida, written left-to-right. Was used in the Philippines in the 13th–18th centuries for the Tagalog language (21 million speakers), which is now written in the Latin script.
