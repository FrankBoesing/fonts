Noto Sans Ogham is an unmodulated (“sans serif”) design for texts in the historical European Ogham script.

Noto Sans Ogham contains 34 glyphs, and supports 33 characters from the Unicode block Ogham.

Supported writing systems

Ogham

Ogham (᚛ᚑᚌᚐᚋ᚜) is a historical European alphabet. Was written bottom-to-top, left-to-right or boustrophedon. Was used in the 5th–10th centuries CE in Ireland, Wales, Devon, Cornwall, and on the Isle of Man, for the Primitive Irish, Old Irish, Pictish, and Old Norse languages. Uses 20 symbols.
