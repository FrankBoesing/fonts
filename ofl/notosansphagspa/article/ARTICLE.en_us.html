Noto Sans PhagsPa is an unmodulated (“sans serif”) design for texts in the historical Central Asian Phags-pa script.

Noto Sans PhagsPa contains 379 glyphs, 5 OpenType features, and supports 94 characters from 3 Unicode blocks: Phags-pa, CJK Symbols and Punctuation, Mongolian.

Supported writing systems

Phags-pa

Phags-pa (ʼPhags-pa, ꡏꡡꡃ ꡣꡡꡙ ꡐꡜꡞ) is a historical Central Asian abugida, written vertically right-to-left. Was sporadically used 1269–1360 in the Yuan empire as a unified script for Mongolian, Tibetan, Sanskrit, Chinese, Persian, Uyghur. Created by the Tibetan monk and State Preceptor Drogön Chögyal Phagpa for Kublai Khan. Read more on ScriptSource, Unicode, Wikipedia, Wiktionary, r12a.
