Noto Sans Ol Chiki is an unmodulated (“sans serif”) design for texts in the Indic Ol Chiki script.

Noto Sans Ol Chiki has multiple weights, contains 55 glyphs, and supports 53 characters from the Unicode block Ol Chiki.

Supported writing systems

Ol Chiki

Ol Chiki (Ol Cemet’, Ol, Santali, ᱚᱞ ᱪᱤᱠᱤ) is an Indic alphabet, written left-to-right. Used in India, Bangladesh and Nepal for Santhali (6 million speakers), alongside Devanagari, Bengali, Oriya and Latin. Created in the 1920s by Pandit Raghunath Murmu. Has 6 vowel and 24 consonant letters. Read more on ScriptSource, Unicode, Wikipedia, Wiktionary, r12a.
