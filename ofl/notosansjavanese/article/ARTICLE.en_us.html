Noto Sans Javanese is an unmodulated (“sans serif”) design for texts in the Southeast Asian Javanese script.

Noto Sans Javanese contains 405 glyphs, 7 OpenType features, and supports 99 characters from the Unicode block Javanese.

Supported writing systems

Javanese

Javanese (Aksara Jawa, ꦄꦏ꧀ꦱꦫꦗꦮ) is a Southeast Asian abugida, written left-to-right. Used since the 15h century for the Javanese language on the Indonesian island of Java. Also used for Sundanese, Madurese, Sasak, Indonesian, Kawi, Sanskrit. Needs software support for complex text layout (shaping). Read more on ScriptSource, Unicode, Wikipedia, Wiktionary, r12a.
